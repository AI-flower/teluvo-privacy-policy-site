Unless required for the services you request, please avoid including personal or sensitive information in prompts, code, logs, files, screenshots, voice input, or other uploaded content.

1. Scope

This Policy applies to products and services we provide through the Teluvo app, our website, related pages, and future service forms based on technical evolution.

This Policy does not apply to third-party products or services. When you use third-party cloud providers, third-party login, payment services, push notification services, model providers, or other external services, those parties process data under their own terms and privacy policies.

2. Information We Collect and Use

We collect and use personal information only as necessary to provide features, comply with legal obligations, protect service security, and improve user experience.

2.1 Registration, Sign-In, and Account Management

When you register, sign in, bind, or manage a Teluvo account, we may collect:

  • Phone number, email address, and verification code
  • Username, nickname, profile photo
  • Third-party account identifiers if you choose third-party sign-in

We use this information for identity verification, account access, security and fraud prevention, account recovery, notifications, and support.

2.2 Cloud Server Setup, Remote Connection, and Deployment

When you use one-click cloud setup, remote connection, deployment, sync, or operations-assistance features, you may submit or authorize us to process:

  • Cloud provider information, including provider name, account identifiers, project or tenant information, region, instance type, image choice, and network configuration
  • Server connection information, including IP address, domain, port, username, SSH public key or private key, access token, API key, and deployment credentials
  • Configuration and task data, including init scripts, environment variables, repository URL, deployment config, task status, error logs, and runtime logs

We process this data to create resources, automate configuration, establish remote connections, execute deployments, synchronize status, troubleshoot issues, support security auditing, and provide support.

Credentials such as access tokens, API keys, SSH keys, and deployment credentials are highly sensitive. We apply enhanced safeguards and process them only as necessary for requested features and legal compliance.

2.3 Intelligent Assistance and User Content

If you use intelligent Q&A, code assistance, command suggestions, log analysis, or config generation features, we may process content you provide or authorize, including:

  • Prompts, questions, code snippets, and config files
  • Terminal output, error messages, and logs
  • Other text, images, audio, video, or files you submit

We use this content to respond to your instructions, generate outputs, execute tasks, sync state, provide support, and maintain service reliability and security.

Unless separately disclosed and authorized where required, we do not use your submitted business code, configurations, logs, or other user content for unrelated marketing, and we do not use identifiable user content for model training.

2.4 Payments and Subscriptions If Applicable

If you purchase paid services, subscriptions, or add-on features, we may process transaction-related data such as order ID, subscription status, payment status, invoicing details, and billing records.

For iOS users, in-app digital purchases are generally processed by Apple through the App Store. We typically do not receive full payment instrument details, such as full card numbers, but we may receive necessary transaction result data, for example purchase success or failure and subscription status, to provide services, handle disputes, and meet accounting and compliance obligations.

2.5 Device, Log, and Security Information

To ensure stable operation, detect abnormal activity, and improve security, we may automatically collect:

  • Device model, OS version, app version, language, time zone, and network status
  • Crash logs, operation logs, diagnostics, performance data, and access timestamps

We use this information for troubleshooting, bug fixes, abuse prevention, security protection, auditing, and optimization.

2.6 Customer Support, Complaints, and Feedback

When you contact support, submit feedback, file a complaint, or request to exercise privacy rights, we may process:

  • Contact information you provide
  • Issue descriptions, screenshots, logs, order info, or account info
  • Other information necessary to process the request

2.7 Device Permissions

To provide certain features, Teluvo may request device permissions with your authorization:

  • Photos/Files: upload config files, logs, screenshots, and attachments
  • Notifications: task status, alerts, and service notices
  • Camera: Teluvo does not require camera access for basic connection features. If future versions include QR scanning, capture, or visual recognition, we will request permission at that time.

You can disable permissions in your device settings. Some related features may stop working, while unrelated features remain available.

6. Data Retention and Storage

We retain personal information only for as long as needed for the purposes described in this Policy, unless a longer period is required by law. Typical retention periods may include:

  • Account information: up to 3 years after account deletion
  • Payment or transaction records if applicable: up to 3 years after transaction completion, or longer if legally required
  • User content: generally up to 30 days after account deletion, unless longer retention is required for disputes, security incidents, abuse prevention, or legal compliance
  • Technical logs/device data: generally up to 1 year after last service interaction
  • Support/feedback data: generally up to 2 years from submission, or longer where needed to resolve disputes

When retention expires, we delete, anonymize, or otherwise process data as permitted by law.
